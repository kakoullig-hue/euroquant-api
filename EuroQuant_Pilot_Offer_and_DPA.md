## PART 2 — DATA PROCESSING TERMS (Ephemeral Processing Clause)

*This clause governs how EuroQuant handles any document you submit for analysis. It is designed to satisfy GDPR and enterprise data-security review.*

### 1. Ephemeral Processing Guarantee

1.1 All documents submitted by the Client ("Input Documents") are processed exclusively **in volatile memory (RAM)** by the EuroQuant analysis engine.

1.2 Input Documents are **never written to persistent storage**, never cached to disk, and never recorded in application logs.

1.3 Upon completion of analysis — or upon any failure or interruption of analysis — each Input Document is **destroyed immediately and irrecoverably**. No copy is retained by EuroQuant in any form.

1.4 The only data retained after analysis is: (a) a one-way **SHA-256 cryptographic hash** of the document, for audit-trail integrity; and (b) the **extracted risk metrics** (e.g. Governance Intensity Score). Neither permits reconstruction of the original document.

### 2. No Training Use

2.1 EuroQuant will **not** use any Input Document, or any data derived from it, to train, fine-tune, or improve any machine-learning model.

2.2 Client data is processed solely to produce the Client's own due-diligence report and for no other purpose.

### 3. Human Review (GDPR Article 22)

3.1 The risk assessment produced by EuroQuant's automated engine constitutes an **input to a human decision**, not an automated decision.

3.2 No report is considered issued, and no report may be relied upon as the sole basis for any investment decision, until it has been **reviewed and signed off by a qualified analyst**.

3.3 This satisfies the requirements of GDPR Article 22 and the CJEU ruling in *Schufa Holding* (C-634/21).

### 4. Confidentiality

4.1 EuroQuant treats all Input Documents and resulting reports as **strictly confidential** and will not disclose them to any third party.

4.2 This applies during the pilot and survives its termination.

### 5. Scope & Term

5.1 These terms apply to the complimentary pilot audit described in Part 1.

5.2 Either party may terminate the pilot at any time. On termination, clauses 1, 2, and 4 survive indefinitely.
